# Anagrams Scoring: Why Long Words Matter More

In Klystora Anagrams, the scoring isn't linear — it's **exponential**. This completely changes your strategy.

## The Scoring System

| Word Length | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| 3 letters | 1 |
| 4 letters | 2 |
| 5 letters | 4 |
| 6 letters | 8 |
| 7 letters | 16 |
| 8+ letters | 32 |

## The Math

One 8-letter word = **32 points**
Thirty-two 3-letter words = **32 points**

But finding one 8-letter word is usually faster than finding thirty-two 3-letter words. **Efficiency matters.**

## Strategy: The "Build Up" Approach

1. **Start with roots** — Find 3-4 letter base words (CAT, ACT, TAC)
2. **Add prefixes/suffixes** — CATS, ACTS, TACTIC
3. **Look for compound patterns** — CATACTIC (not a word, but you get the idea)

## Common High-Value Patterns

These letter combinations often produce 6+ letter words:
- **-ING** endings
- **-TION** / **-SION**
- **UN-** / **RE-** / **DE-** prefixes
- **Double letters** (LL, SS, TT)

## The Pangram Bonus

If you find a word using ALL the available letters, you get a **50-point pangram bonus**. This single word can be 30-50% of your total score.
