# How to Solve Crosswords Faster: 7 Techniques

Whether you're tackling the NYT crossword or our [Mini Crossword](/mini-crossword), these techniques will sharpen your solving skills.

## 1. Start with the Fill-in-the-Blanks

Clues with blanks ("___ of the Tiger") are usually the easiest. Knock these out first to build your grid foundation.

## 2. Look for Plurals and Verb Endings

If the answer ends in "S", the clue is likely plural. If it ends in "ED", look for past tense. These patterns eliminate half the possibilities instantly.

## 3. Use Crossing Letters

Stuck on a down clue? Solve the across clues that cross it first. Even 2-3 letters can make the answer obvious.

## 4. Beware of Question Marks

In crosswords, a question mark means wordplay. "Head of state?" might be "SCALP" (head of "state").

## 5. Learn the Short Words

These appear in almost every puzzle:
- **ERA**, **EON**, **AGE** (time periods)
- **ALE**, **TEA**, **SODA** (drinks)
- **OREO**, **ARA**, **EMU** (crossword-ese)

## 6. Think About the Constructor's Mindset

Crossword constructors need clean grids. If a section looks impossible, you probably have one letter wrong. Backtrack.

Our [5×5 mini crosswords](/mini-crossword) are perfect for daily practice. 5 minutes, one puzzle, zero commitment.
