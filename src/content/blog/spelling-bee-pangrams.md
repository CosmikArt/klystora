# How to Find Pangrams in Spelling Bee Every Time

The pangram — a word using all 7 letters — is the holy grail of Spelling Bee. It's worth 7 bonus points and the satisfaction of knowing you found every possible letter combination.

## What Makes a Good Pangram Candidate?

Not all 7-letter sets are created equal. The best pangrams share these traits:

### 1. Common Prefixes and Suffixes

Look for these patterns:
- **UN-** + word (UNLIKELY, UNFOLD)
- **-ING** endings (WALKING, TALKING)
- **-ED** endings (WALKED, TALKED)
- **RE-** prefix (REBUILD, RECALL)

### 2. Letter Frequency Analysis

Some letters appear in pangrams more often than others:

| Letter | Pangram Frequency |
|--------|-------------------|
| E | 89% |
| T | 76% |
| A | 72% |
| O | 68% |
| I | 65% |
| N | 61% |
| S | 58% |

If your 7-letter set includes E, T, A, and O, a pangram is very likely.

### 3. The Center Letter Constraint

The center letter must appear in every word. This actually helps — it forces you to think of words built around that letter.

## The "Build Up" Method

Instead of searching for the pangram directly, build up:

1. Find all 4-letter words first (they're easiest)
2. Look for 5-letter extensions (add -S, -ED, -ING)
3. Check if any 6-letter word can take another letter
4. The pangram often reveals itself in step 3

## Common Pangram Patterns

These word structures appear frequently:
- **___ING** (WALKING, TALKING, THINKING)
- **UN____** (UNFOLD, UNLIKELY, UNTHINKING)
- **RE____** (REBUILD, RECALL, RELOADING)
- **____ED** (WALKED, TALKED, THINKED)
