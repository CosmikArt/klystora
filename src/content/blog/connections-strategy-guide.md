# Connections Strategy: How to Solve Without Guessing

Connections puzzles look simple — 16 words, 4 groups of 4. But the difficulty curve is brutal. Here's how to approach them systematically.

## The Four Difficulty Levels

Connections groups are ordered by difficulty:

1. **🟨 Yellow** — Straightforward category (colors, animals)
2. **🟩 Green** — Slightly tricky (things that are "cold", verbs that mean "to leave")
3. **🟦 Blue** — Wordplay or obscure knowledge
4. **🟪 Purple** — The diabolical one (words that follow "___ ball", things with rings)

## Phase 1: The Obvious Scan (30 seconds)

Read all 16 words once. Look for:
- **Colors** (RED, BLUE, GREEN, GOLD)
- **Animals** (BEAR, LION, SHARK, WOLF)
- **Numbers** (ONE, TWO, THREE, FOUR)
- **Body parts** (HEAD, HAND, FOOT, BACK)

If you spot an obvious group, **don't lock it in yet**. Verify all 4 words fit perfectly.

## Phase 2: Part of Speech Sorting

Group words by grammatical function:
- **Nouns** (can you put "a" or "the" before it?)
- **Verbs** (can you put "to" before it?)
- **Adjectives** (can you put "very" before it?)

This often reveals hidden connections. "BREAK", "CRACK", "SPLIT", "TEAR" can all be verbs meaning "to separate."

## Phase 3: The "Can It Follow/Precede X?" Test

Many purple groups work like this:
- **___ BALL** (FOOT, BASE, BASKET, MEAT)
- **FIRE ___** (TRUCK, DRILL, STATION, WALL)
- **___ FLY** (BUTTER, DRAGON, HOUSE, FIRE)

Test each word against common prefixes/suffixes.

## Phase 4: Process of Elimination

If you have 12 words left and need 3 groups of 4, look for:
- Words that clearly DON'T belong together
- Words that have only one possible connection
- The "odd one out" that reveals a hidden group

## Common Traps

- **Red herrings**: Words that seem to fit multiple categories
- **Thematic overlap**: "APPLE" could be fruit OR tech company
- **Obscure references**: Pop culture, geography, or history knowledge required
